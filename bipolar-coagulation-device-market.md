<p><ul><li>General Frequency</li><li>High Frequency</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bipolar coagulation device market is categorized into general frequency and high-frequency types. General frequency devices operate at lower frequencies, providing controlled tissue heating and minimizing thermal spread, making them suitable for delicate procedures. In contrast, high-frequency devices utilize elevated frequencies for rapid tissue heating, ensuring effective coagulation in larger volumes. Both types are essential in surgical environments, each catering to specific procedural requirements based on precision, tissue type, and desired outcomes in hemostasis and surgical efficiency.</p></p>
