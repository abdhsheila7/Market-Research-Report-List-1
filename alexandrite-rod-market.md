<p><p>The Alexandrite Rod market encompasses two primary production methods: the Czochralski Method and the Float-Zone Method. The Czochralski Method involves melting a material and drawing a single crystal from the molten mass, providing high purity and uniformity. In contrast, the Float-Zone Method uses a zone melting technique where a rod is passed through a heated zone to create a high-quality crystal without contamination. Both methods are essential for producing alexandrite with distinct optical properties, catering to various industrial and gemstone applications.</p></p>
