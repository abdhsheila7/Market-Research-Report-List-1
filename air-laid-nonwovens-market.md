<p><ul><li>Wet-laid Nonwovens</li><li>Dry-laid Nonwovens</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Air-laid nonwovens, wet-laid nonwovens, and dry-laid nonwovens are three primary types in the nonwoven fabric market. </p><p>Air-laid nonwovens use a dry fiber process, creating a fluffy, absorbent material ideal for hygiene products and wipes. Wet-laid nonwovens involve dispersing fibers in water, producing strong and uniform fabrics suitable for filtration and industrial applications. Dry-laid nonwovens utilize mechanical bonding of dry fibers, resulting in versatile fabrics for various uses, including insulation and geotextiles, leveraging the strength and lightweight characteristics of the materials.</p></p>
