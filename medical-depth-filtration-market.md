<p><p>The medical depth filtration market encompasses various filter types, including cellulose, activated carbon, diatomaceous earth (DE), and others. Cellulose filters are primarily used for biological applications, offering excellent retention of microorganisms. Activated carbon filters excel in removing organic contaminants and odors, enhancing purification. Diatomaceous earth is prized for its high surface area and porosity, making it effective for fine filtration. Other market types may include synthetic materials and polymer-based filters, each tailored to specific medical and pharmaceutical applications.</p></p>
